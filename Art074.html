Glitch Art

For this project I used Photoshop, Audacity, and Illustrator. I first collected images I felt reflected my topic of Nancy Burson and Facial Recognition Software. I compiled these images and created a digital composite which I then put into Audacity. In Audacity I used echo, invert, fade in, fade out, and reverb to achieve my glitch effect. Then I created a vector image in illustrator to incorporate into my image, taking inspiration from the way that facial recognition software measures your face with lines, creating an abstract topographic image of a face.

Hyper Reality

This project was in part inspired by Keiichi Matsuda's "HYPER-REALITY". I used After Effects as well as Illustrator and Photoshop in order to achieve my final result. Originally I had only planned on including the three images that disappear behind the train, but I felt that didn't quite fill the video nor did it make enough edits for me to consider the final product a piece I had created. So, I began to fill the piece with more of my own work, adding a piece I made previously in this class as well as a graphic I had made. I had planned on adding an animation of mine in, however, I was unable to import a GIF into After Effects, so I instead went with a graphic.

Net Art

This project was inspired mostly by http://mirrrroring.net/, though also by general by collage art. Originally I was focusing on kitchen appliances and food, though as it developed I found more images for food, and so I let go of the kitchen appliance aspect. I mostly wanted to create a chaotic field of food, where if you click around enough you can find different links to separate sites.

3D Mashup

For this project I was inspired by many different science fiction stories, the idea of mixing species to create a sort of chimera, and the idea of forcing skeletons from different species into an animal. For this, I took the skull of a deer and the skeleton on a Wooley Mammoth and mashed them into to body of a dog. Though this animal has the spine of a wooly mammoth sticking out of its back, as well as it's tusks out of its neck, and it seems to be wearing the skull of a deer over its head, I imagined it being alive. A sort of representation of what could go wrong in a science experiment.

Code As Art

For this project I wanted to do something that looked a little softer and cleaner. I wanted to keep it interactive but use softer colors and shaped. I got rid of any sharp lines and used more muted colors. I wanted movement in the piece even without interaction, so I used what we learned in some of our in class assignments and created two objects that move across the page. In addition to this, there is a square that follows your curser throughout the piece, which once clicked will turn into a circle and four other circles will appear throughout the piece.
